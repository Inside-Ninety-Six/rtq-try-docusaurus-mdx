<div class='question question'>

The number of students absent from Year $7$ was recorded as follows.

| Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday |
|:------:|:-------:|:---------:|:--------:|:------:|
| $4$    | $6$     | $5$       | $3$      | $?$    |

If the **mean number** of students absent that week from Year $7$, was $4$, how many students were absent on Friday?

</div>
<div class='workings'>
<div class='working'>

Let the number of students absent on Friday $= a$

$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Mean number of absent students}   &= \dfrac {\text{Total absent students}} {\text{Total days}} \\\\
4                                       &= \dfrac {4 + 6 + 5 + 3 + a} {5} \\\\
4 \times 5                              &= 18 + a \\\\
20                                      &= 18 + a \\\\
18 + a                                  &= 20 \\\\
a                                       &= 20 - 18 \\\\
a                                       &= 2
\end{aligned}
$

Number of students absent on Friday $= 2$

</div>
</div>
<div class='answers'>
<div class='answer'>

$2$

</div>
</div>

<div class='question question'>

What is the average of $\frac {1}{2}$ and $\frac {1}{3}$ ?

</div>
<div class='workings'>
<div class='working'>

$
\begin{aligned}
&= \dfrac{ \left ( \dfrac{1} {2} + \dfrac {1} {3} \right )} {2} \\\\
&= \dfrac{ \left ( \dfrac{3} {2 \times 3} + \dfrac {2} {3 \times 2} \right )} {2} \\\\
&= \dfrac{ \left ( \dfrac{3} {6} + \dfrac {2} {6} \right )} {2} \\\\
&= \dfrac{\dfrac{5} {6}} {2} \\\\
&= \dfrac{5} {12}
\end{aligned}
$

</div>
</div>
<div class='answers'>
<div class='answer'>

$\dfrac{5} {12}$

</div>
