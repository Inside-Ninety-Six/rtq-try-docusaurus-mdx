                                                                                        $ \begin{aligned} j &= \pounds 2.00 \\ j &= 200 \text{p} \\ l &= 65 \ \text{p} \\ T &= \pounds 4.65 \\ \text{or,} T &= 465 \ \text{p} \\ Jam &= \dfrac {1}{5} \times \text{weight} \\ \end{aligned} $

                                                                                        Let's take weight as $m$ and substitute

                                                                                        $ \begin{aligned} 200 + 65 + \dfrac {1}{5} \times m &= 465 \\ 265 + \dfrac {1}{5} \times m &= 465 \\ \dfrac {1}{5} \times m &= 465-265 \\ \dfrac {1}{5} \times m &= 200 \times 5 \\ m &= 1000 \text{g} \ \end{aligned} $

                                                                                        $1000 \ \text{g}$
